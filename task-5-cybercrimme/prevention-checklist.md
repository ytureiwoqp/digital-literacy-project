Stay Safe Online: Student Edition
Follow these 8 actionable steps to protect your digital life:

   Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Use app-based authenticators (like Google Authenticator) for your Gmail and social media accounts. Never rely on SMS alone.

   The "PIN is for Giving, Not Receiving" Rule: Remember that you never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If someone asks you to enter a PIN for a "refund" or "prize," it is a scam.

   Verify UPI IDs: Before hitting 'Send' on apps like GPay or PhonePe, double check the verified name of the recipient. Scammers often use names like "Customer Care" to look official.

   Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking: Never log into your bank account or shopping sites using college canteen or railway station Wi-Fi. Use your mobile data instead.

   Check the URL: Before entering any password, ensure the website address is correct. Look for https:// and be wary of extra hyphens or misspellings (e.g., instagramm.com).

   Audit App Permissions: Regularly check which apps have access to your contacts, SMS, and microphone. Delete "cleaner" or "battery saver" apps that request excessive permissions.

   Use Strong, Unique Passwords: Don't use your birthday or "Name123." Use a password manager to keep unique strings for every site so a leak on one doesn't compromise all.

   Think Before You Click: If an email or DM creates a sense of panic or offers a deal that seems "too good to be true," it probably is. Take five minutes to breathe and verify through an official channel.

🚨 How to Report a Crime
   If you or someone you know falls victim to a cybercrime:

Immediate Action: Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline at 1930.

Official Complaint: Register your complaint at the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in/Webform/Accept.aspx .
