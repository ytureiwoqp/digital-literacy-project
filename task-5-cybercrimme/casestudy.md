The "Urgent KYC Update" Trap
Phishing is a deceptive practice where attackers masquerade as a trusted entity to steal sensitive information such as login credentials, Aadhaar numbers, or banking details. In the context of Indian college students, this often manifests as a "Sense of Urgency" scam.

How it Happens (Step-by-Step):

   The Bait: The student receives an SMS or WhatsApp message claiming to be from a well-known bank (e.g., SBI or HDFC). The message warns that their account/KYC will be suspended within 24 hours if they don't "verify" their details immediately.

   The Hook: The message contains a link to a website that looks identical to the official banking portal, often using a URL like secure-hfdc-kyc.com instead of the legitimate domain.

   The Catch: On the fake site, the student enters their Customer ID and password. The site then prompts for an OTP (One-Time Password) sent to their phone.

   The Theft: In real-time, the attacker uses the captured credentials on the real banking site. When the student enters the OTP into the fake site, they are actually handing over the final key for the attacker to authorize a high-value transfer.

Target Audience:
While anyone can be targeted, students are often vulnerable due to their frequent use of digital services and potential lack of experience with formal banking procedures.

Consequences:
The immediate result is total financial loss from the linked account. Furthermore, the stolen credentials can be used for Identity Theft, allowing criminals to take out fraudulent loans or open "mule accounts" in the student's name, leading to long-term legal complications.
